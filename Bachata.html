Bachata is a dance originally from the Dominican Republic and one of the most popular dances from Latin America.

The dance was influenced by merengue and salsa and has been on rise since the late 2000s, when bachata sensual was created and quickly took place alongside bachata urbana and bachata dominicana. Bachata sensual evolved under the initiative of Korke & Judith from Spain and led to a huge increase in the popularity of bachata. The dance has become more lyrical and modern, also with the rise of remixes and more popular bachata songs by artists such as Romeo Santos, Aventura, Prince Royce, Dani J and others.

The characteristics of bachata sensual are body waves, body isolations, involvement of arm styling and many soft movements that use the principle of a very fine leading & following.



Why Bachata?

Benefits of dancing Bachata

Joy and happiness

Dancing improves self confidence and stimulates the release of important chemicals, like dopamine, oxytocin and endorphin. Bachata dancing has been found to be a very good anti-stress therapy. There is nothing better than a nice bachata party on Friday night after a long weekend of work.

Social bonding

Bachata dancing is a great way to meet lots of fun and interesting people. It brings together people of different nationalities, origins and background. Also here in Frankfurt the bachata community is very strong and there are weekly dance parties as regular meet-ups of friends. Come along, make new friends and enjoy dancing!

Health enhancement

Bachata is a semi-dynamic dance style. As much as it helps to reduce weight, improves heart and cardiovascular system, it is also a dance that requires body control and use of core and leg muscles, among others, which imrpoves overall flexibility and strength.

Memory improvement

According to many studies, dancing improves visual perception and spacial memory and helps to prevent diseases such as Dementia and Alzheimer.

About us

Hi! We are Eduardo and Natalia
the founders of Alma Dance

We are an international couple from Mexico and Slovakia living in Frankfurt. As passionate dancers, and after a several years teaching latin dance, we decided to start a project to give latin dance classes and build a strong dance community with an all-rounded dance offering. And so Alma Dance came to life, representing the way we see dance and the place it has in our lives (in Spanish, “alma” means “soul”). As professional dance teachers and performers fluent in different languages, we teach both locally and internationally.
